# 🧑‍🏫 Facilitator Guide: Module 7 – Optimizing GitHub Repositories for AI

## ⏱️ Duration
**60 minutes total**

---

## 👩‍💻 Audience
Software engineers seeking to maximize the effectiveness of AI tools in their codebase.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, learners will:
✅ Audit and refactor repositories for optimal AI tool effectiveness.
✅ Apply standards for type safety, documentation, and modularity.
✅ Use rules files (CLAUDE.md, rules.mdc) to capture best practices.
✅ Practice iterative improvement: update rules and structure when friction occurs.

---

## 🗺️ Content Flow

### 1. **Kickoff & Repo Audit Overview (10 min)**
- Explain the problem: “Messy repos lead to messy AI suggestions.”
- Reintroduce the **CLAUDE.md** repo rules, type safety, modularization, and doc standards.
- Share sample audit checklist and “before/after” code examples.

---

### 2. **Repo Audit & Improvement (25 min)**
- Learners:
  1. Use a provided checklist to audit their (or a sample) repo for AI-readiness:
     - TypeScript usage, modular structure, documentation, separation of DTOs/types, scripts.
  2. Identify at least five areas for improvement.
  3. Apply at least three best practices (type defs, modularity, doc improvements) to a real module/component.

---

### 3. **Rules File Exercise (10 min)**
- Learners add or update a **DEVELOPMENT_RULES.md** or similar rules file, documenting:
  - Any new rules they followed or created
  - How to avoid an error/failure they encountered during refactor
  - “Meta rule”: When an error is found, update the rules file

---

### 4. **Sharing & Reflection (10 min)**
- Group shares: Before/after repo structure, rules added, biggest “aha” from the audit.
- Facilitator highlights how these improvements enable smarter AI support and faster onboarding for new collaborators.

## 🎉 Outcomes
Participants will:
✅ Audit and improve a repo’s AI context-awareness.
✅ Apply and document best practices.
✅ Submit a refactored module/component with clear rules and standards.

---

## 📌 Key Talking Points
- AI suggestions are only as good as your repo structure.
- Rules files = onboarding for both humans and AI agents.
- Iterative rule updates lead to long-term productivity gains.

---

## 💬 Reflection Questions
- What was the most surprising repo issue uncovered?
- How did documentation or rules improve your workflow?
- How can AI tools support repo hygiene and best practices?
